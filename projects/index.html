Rainbow Six: Siege Randomizer

I developed a randomizer for the first person shooter Ranbow Six:Siege. The program randomly generates an operator along with a set of valid loadout. I used an object-oriented approach in the design process for the ease of making future changes in case of new patchs.

Spam Email Detector

In the summer of 2023, I developed a spam email detector via prompt engineering with OpenAI's GPT-3.5 and GPT-3.5-turbo models. During this process, I formulated and fine-tuned GPT prompts, with the goal of generating precise one-word prediction to classify incoming emails into either spam or non-spam groups.

I continuously evaluated model performance and optimized the prompts along the way, ensuring consistent improvement in the detector's accuracy and efficiency.

Senior Thesis

For my undergraduate senior thesis project, I joined Prof. Jae Hur's lab to research an obesity-related gene for its impact on lipid metabolism. The lab focused utilized Drosophila Melanogaster (fruit flies) as the model organism.

In this process, I managed transgenic fruit fly populations with genetic knockdowns and conducted DNA isolation and RT-qPCR, and utilized spectrophotometry to quantify triglyceride and protein levels, with data analysis in R and Excel.
